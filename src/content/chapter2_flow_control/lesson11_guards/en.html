The if keyword can be used with case expressions to add a guard to a pattern. A guard is an expression that must evaluate to True for the pattern to match.

Only a limited set of operators can be used in guards:

  • == - equals
  • != - not equals
  • > - greater than (Int)
  • >= - greater than or equal to (Int)
  • < - less than (Int)
  • <= - less than or equal to (Int)
  • >. - greater than (Float)
  • >=. - greater than or equal to (Float)
  • <. - less than (Float)
  • <=. - less than or equal to (Float)
  • + - addition (Int)
  • +. - addition (Float)
  • - - subtraction (Int)
  • -. - subtraction (Float)
  • * - multiplication (Int)
  • *. - multiplication (Float)
  • \ - division (Int)
  • \. - division (Float)
  • gleam/int/remainder - remainder function (Int)
  • || - Or
  • && - And
  • ! - Not
  • some_var - variables
  • const_var - constant
  • tuple.0 - tuple index
  • record_type.field - field access
functions cannot be called at all.
